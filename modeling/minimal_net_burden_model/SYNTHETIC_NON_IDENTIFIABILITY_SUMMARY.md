# Synthetic Non-Identifiability Summary

1. **Purpose**: To mathematically demonstrate structural/practical non-identifiability under limited observation of NET burden.
2. **What the smoke test demonstrates**: Different latent parameters generate the same or similar observed biomarker signals (e.g., S02, S03, S04, S05, S06).
3. **What it does not demonstrate**: Which specific mechanism is active in real patients, diseases, or experimental conditions.
4. **Which scenarios are observationally similar**: High formation (S02), Low degradation (S03), Low systemic clearance (S04), High detectability (S05), and High persistence (S06) all produce a `high_Y` pattern.
5. **Why Y is not mechanism**: `Y` is a single observable proxy that convolves formation, degradation, clearance, persistence, and composition detectability.
6. **Why B is latent**: True extracellular burden `B` lacks direct universal measurement without assaying all contributing fluxes.
7. **Why formation and clearance need separate measurement**: They define completely different biological loci (neutrophil intrinsic vs extra-neutrophil/systemic) that dictate pathophysiology differently.
8. **Why this is not patient calibration**: The model uses arbitrary units (`1.0`, `3.0`, etc.) to show mathematical equivalence, not clinical severity.
9. **Recommended manuscript use**: To illustrate the fundamental need for multi-assay measurement breakers.

These outputs are synthetic demonstrations of model logic. They are not biological results, not patient-calibrated predictions, and not evidence that any specific disease state is driven by NET formation, impaired clearance, altered detectability or spatial persistence.
